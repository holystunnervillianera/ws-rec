## Part 1: Core Master Prompts

### 1. The Viral Loop Designer: Growth Mechanics Engine

**Purpose:** Design viral loops and product-led growth mechanisms

```
ROLE
You are a growth strategist who has designed viral loops at companies like 
Slack, Dropbox, and Figma. You understand how products naturally spread.

GOAL
Design a viral loop for [PRODUCT/FEATURE] that drives [GROWTH METRIC] 
through [MECHANISM].

INPUTS I WILL PROVIDE
- Product description and core value proposition
- Target user and their pain point
- Current user acquisition cost (if known)
- Viral coefficient target (if known)
- Constraints (budget, timeline, technical feasibility)

NON-NEGOTIABLE RULES
- The viral mechanism must be natural, not forced
- Users must benefit from inviting others
- The loop must be measurable and trackable
- Avoid spam-like or manipulative mechanics
- Design for both new users and existing users
- Consider friction at each step of the loop

PROCESS
Step 1: Identify the core value that makes users want to share
Step 2: Design the sharing mechanism (how do they invite?)
Step 3: Design the incentive (why do they invite?)
Step 4: Design the onboarding for invited users
Step 5: Calculate viral coefficient and payback period

OUTPUT FORMAT
Viral Loop Overview: [How it works in 1 paragraph]
Loop Mechanics: [Step-by-step breakdown]
Sharing Mechanism: [Exactly how users share]
Incentive Structure: [Why they share]
Viral Coefficient: [Expected K-factor]
Payback Period: [Time to recover CAC]
Implementation Roadmap: [How to build it]
Metrics to Track: [What to measure]
```

## Part 2: Specialized Modules (Use with Core Prompts)

### Module A: B2B vs. B2C Adjuster

Use this when strategy needs to account for customer type:

```
B2B VS. B2C ADJUSTER

For B2B (selling to businesses):
- Focus on ROI and business impact
- Sales cycle is longer (3-6 months typical)
- Multiple stakeholders involved in decision
- Pricing is often negotiable
- Retention is critical (high switching cost)
- Viral growth is limited (fewer buyers)

For B2C (selling to consumers):
- Focus on ease of use and emotional appeal
- Sales cycle is short (minutes to days)
- Single decision maker (usually)
- Pricing is fixed
- Retention is harder (low switching cost)
- Viral growth is possible (many potential users)

For B2B2C (selling through businesses to consumers):
- Balance both B2B and B2C considerations
- B2B sales and support is critical
- B2C virality amplifies through B2B partners
- Pricing is often tiered (per-user or per-transaction)
- Retention depends on both B2B and B2C satisfaction

Adjust your strategy based on customer type.
```

### Module B: Early Stage vs. Scale Stage Adjuster

Use this when strategy depends on company stage:

```
EARLY STAGE VS. SCALE STAGE ADJUSTER

For early stage (< 10k users, < $1M ARR):
- Focus on product-market fit, not growth
- Prioritize learning over optimization
- Use low-cost, high-touch acquisition
- Retention is more important than acquisition
- Viral growth is unlikely (too small)
- Pricing may be free or freemium

For growth stage (10k-100k users, $1M-$10M ARR):
- Focus on repeatable acquisition channels
- Optimize conversion and retention
- Start paid acquisition (PPC, sales)
- Build viral loops and referral programs
- Experiment with pricing and monetization
- Expand to new markets or segments

For scale stage (> 100k users, > $10M ARR):
- Focus on efficiency and profitability
- Optimize unit economics relentlessly
- Expand to new channels and markets
- Build enterprise sales and support
- Invest in brand and thought leadership
- Explore M&A and partnership opportunities

Adjust your strategy based on company stage.
```

### Module C: Competitive Intensity Adjuster

Use this when market competition is a factor:

```
COMPETITIVE INTENSITY ADJUSTER

In uncontested markets (low competition):
- Focus on product quality and user experience
- Build brand loyalty and community
- Expand to adjacent use cases
- Optimize for profitability early
- Invest in thought leadership

In competitive markets (medium competition):
- Focus on differentiation and positioning
- Compete on speed and execution
- Build switching costs (integrations, data lock-in)
- Invest in customer success and retention
- Build network effects where possible

In hyper-competitive markets (high competition):
- Focus on niche positioning and dominance
- Compete on price or features aggressively
- Build strong brand and community
- Invest heavily in acquisition and retention
- Look for adjacent markets with less competition

Adjust your strategy based on competitive intensity.
```

### Module D: Budget Constraint Optimizer

Use this when budget is limited:

```
BUDGET CONSTRAINT OPTIMIZER

If marketing budget is < $10k/month:
- Focus on organic and viral growth
- Use content marketing and SEO
- Leverage community and partnerships
- Use founder-led sales and marketing
- Avoid paid advertising (low ROI at scale)

If marketing budget is $10k-$100k/month:
- Mix organic and paid acquisition
- Test multiple paid channels
- Invest in content and SEO
- Build community and partnerships
- Optimize unit economics relentlessly

If marketing budget is > $100k/month:
- Scale paid acquisition across channels
- Invest in brand building
- Build enterprise sales team
- Expand to new markets and segments
- Optimize for profitability and LTV

Adjust your strategy to fit within budget constraints.
```

### Module E: International Expansion Planner

Use this when expanding to new markets:

```
INTERNATIONAL EXPANSION PLANNER

For English-speaking markets (US, UK, Australia):
- Minimal localization needed
- Similar payment and legal requirements
- Similar marketing and sales approaches
- Focus on differentiation vs. competitors

For European markets (Germany, France, Spain):
- Localization required (language, culture)
- GDPR compliance mandatory
- Different payment methods (SEPA, iDEAL)
- Different marketing and sales approaches

For Asian markets (India, China, Japan, Southeast Asia):
- Significant localization required
- Different payment methods and platforms
- Different competitive landscape
- Different user expectations and behaviors

For emerging markets:
- Limited payment infrastructure
- Price sensitivity is high
- Mobile-first approach required
- Partnership-based distribution often needed

Plan your expansion strategy based on target market.
```

### Module F: Product-Led Growth Enabler

Use this when designing product-led growth:

```
PRODUCT-LED GROWTH ENABLER

For product-led growth to work:
- Product must be easy to try (free trial or freemium)
- Core value must be obvious within minutes
- Onboarding must be fast and intuitive
- Upgrade path must be clear and compelling
- Viral loop must be built into product
- Support must be self-service (docs, in-app help)

Design your product for self-serve adoption:
- Remove friction from signup and onboarding
- Show value in first 5 minutes
- Make upgrade decision obvious
- Build in sharing and referral mechanics
- Create in-app education and guidance
- Measure and optimize onboarding funnel

Build your go-to-market around product:
- Use product as primary acquisition channel
- Minimize sales and marketing overhead
- Focus on retention and expansion revenue
- Build community around product
- Use product usage data to guide decisions

Apply product-led growth principles to your strategy.
```

### Module G: Network Effects Architect

Use this when building network effects:

```
NETWORK EFFECTS ARCHITECT

For direct network effects (value increases with users):
- Focus on critical mass (minimum viable network)
- Subsidize early users to reach critical mass
- Create exclusive communities or tiers
- Build switching costs through data and integrations
- Expand to adjacent use cases

For indirect network effects (value increases through ecosystem):
- Build platform for third-party developers
- Create marketplace for complementary products
- Invest in partner success and enablement
- Create network of integrations
- Build community of builders

For two-sided network effects (value for both sides):
- Balance supply and demand carefully
- Subsidize the supply side initially
- Create quality controls and reputation systems
- Build trust and safety mechanisms
- Expand to adjacent use cases

Design your product and go-to-market to build network effects.
```

### Module H: Data-Driven Decision Maker

Use this when making growth decisions:

```
DATA-DRIVEN DECISION MAKER

For every growth decision, ask:
- What data do we have about this?
- What data are we missing?
- How confident are we in this decision?
- What's the cost of being wrong?
- How quickly can we test this?

For each experiment:
- Define success metrics upfront
- Calculate required sample size
- Run test long enough for statistical significance
- Analyze results with proper statistical methods
- Document learnings and next steps

For each metric:
- Understand what it measures
- Know the benchmark for your industry
- Track trends over time (not just snapshots)
- Segment by user cohort, channel, geography
- Create alerts for anomalies

Make decisions based on data, not intuition.
```

## Part 4: Quick Reference Frameworks

### The Growth Metrics Dashboard

Track these metrics for every product:

| Metric | Formula | Target | Frequency |
|--------|---------|--------|-----------|
| **CAC** | Total acquisition spend / New customers | < LTV/3 | Monthly |
| **LTV** | Average revenue per user × Average lifetime | > CAC × 3 | Monthly |
| **Payback Period** | CAC / Monthly ARPU | < 12 months | Monthly |
| **Viral Coefficient** | Invites per user × Conversion rate | > 1.0 | Monthly |
| **Retention (D7)** | Active users day 7 / New users day 1 | > 40% | Weekly |
| **Retention (D30)** | Active users day 30 / New users day 1 | > 20% | Weekly |
| **Churn Rate** | Lost customers / Starting customers | < 5% | Monthly |
| **NPS** | Promoters - Detractors / Total | > 50 | Quarterly |

### The Viral Loop Formula

```
Viral Coefficient (K) = Invites per user × Conversion rate of invites

If K > 1: Exponential growth (viral)
If K = 1: Linear growth (sustainable)
If K < 1: Declining growth (needs acquisition)

Payback Period = CAC / (Monthly ARPU × K)
```
